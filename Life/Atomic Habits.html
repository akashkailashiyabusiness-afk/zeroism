Atomic Habits

Core Concepts

The 1% Rule

Small improvements compound over time.

Example: 1% better daily = 37x better in a year.

Habit Loop

Cue → Craving → Response → Reward.

Example: Phone buzz (cue) → Check it (response).

Identity-Based Habits

Focus on who you want to become.

Example: “I’m a reader” vs. “I’ll read a book.”

Four Laws of Behavior Change

1. Make it Obvious

Cue habits with environment design.

Example: Place running shoes by the door.

2. Make it Attractive

Pair habits with enjoyment.

Example: Listen to music while exercising.

3. Make it Easy

Reduce friction to start.

Example: Start with 2 push-ups.

4. Make it Satisfying

Reward yourself to reinforce.

Example: Check off a habit tracker.

Actionable Strategies

Tiny Habits

Start small with the 2-minute rule.

  • “Run for 30 minutes” → “Put on running shoes”
  • “Read a book” → “Read one page”

Habit Stacking

Link new habits to existing ones.

  • After coffee → Meditate 1 minute
  • After brushing → Do 5 squats

Track & Reflect

Monitor progress and adjust.

  • Use a habit tracker app or paper
  • Review weekly: What’s working?
