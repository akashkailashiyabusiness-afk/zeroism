Conversational Intelligence

"How Great Leaders Build Trust and Get Extraordinary Results"

Book Overview

*Conversational Intelligence: How Great Leaders Build Trust and Get Extraordinary Results* by Judith E. Glaser, published in 2013, is a groundbreaking book that explores the power of conversations in leadership and organizational success. Glaser, a renowned organizational anthropologist, introduces the concept of "Conversational Intelligence" (C-IQ), which emphasizes the role of trust, neuroscience, and effective communication in building strong relationships and achieving exceptional results.

The book cover features a striking design with large quotation marks framing the title, symbolizing the focus on dialogue. The subtitle is highlighted in bold orange, and a quote from Daniel H. Pink, author of *Drive* and *To Sell Is Human*, praises the book: "If you’re not getting the results you want, maybe it’s time to give your conversational intelligence a boost." This sets the tone for a practical, transformative guide for leaders, teams, and individuals.

Key Concepts

The Three Levels of Conversation

Glaser identifies three levels of conversation that determine the quality of interactions:

  • Level I: Transactional - This level involves exchanging information ("telling and asking"). It’s often directive and lacks deep connection, focusing on efficiency over relationship-building.
  • Level II: Positional - Here, conversations are about advocating and inquiring, often involving persuasion or defending a point of view. It can lead to conflict if not handled with care.
  • Level III: Transformational - The highest level, where co-creation and sharing occur. These conversations foster trust, innovation, and mutual understanding, leading to breakthroughs.

Trust and the Neuroscience of Conversations

Glaser integrates neuroscience to explain how conversations impact the brain. Distrust triggers the amygdala, releasing cortisol and creating a "fight or flight" response, while trust activates the prefrontal cortex, releasing oxytocin, which fosters collaboration and openness. Leaders who understand this can shift conversations from fear-based to trust-based, improving team dynamics.

Conversational Dashboard

The "Conversational Dashboard" is a tool Glaser introduces to assess and improve conversations. It helps individuals gauge the level of trust, clarity, and engagement in their interactions, enabling them to adjust their approach for better outcomes.

Important Lessons

  • Build Trust Through Listening: Active listening and validating others’ perspectives create a safe space for dialogue, reducing defensiveness and fostering collaboration.
  • Shift from "I" to "We": Transformational conversations focus on co-creation, moving away from individual agendas to collective goals, which strengthens teams.
  • Be Aware of Conversational Blind Spots: Misunderstandings often arise from assumptions or lack of awareness. Glaser encourages leaders to ask discovery questions to uncover hidden perspectives.
  • Use "And" Instead of "But": Small linguistic shifts, like replacing "but" with "and," can make conversations more inclusive and less confrontational, encouraging openness.

Why Read This Book?

*Conversational Intelligence* is a must-read for leaders, managers, and anyone looking to improve their communication skills. Judith E. Glaser provides actionable strategies grounded in neuroscience, making the book both practical and insightful. Whether you’re navigating workplace challenges or seeking to build stronger personal relationships, this book offers tools to elevate your conversations and achieve extraordinary results. Fans of leadership books like *Leaders Eat Last* by Simon Sinek or *Dare to Lead* by Brené Brown will find this a valuable addition to their library.
