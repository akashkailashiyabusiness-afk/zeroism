🧠 Psychology Models

  • Incentive-Caused Bias

    The universal tendency to act in one's own self-interest, often unconsciously, due to the incentives in place. Never underestimate its power.

    Learn More

  • Consistency Principle

    People have a deep need to be seen as consistent. As such, once they have publicly committed to something, they are much more likely to follow through.

    Learn More

  • Social Proof

    The tendency to assume the actions of others reflect the correct behavior for a given situation. If everyone is doing it, it must be right.

    Learn More

  • Sunk Cost Fallacy

    The tendency to continue an endeavor because of previously invested resources (time, money, effort), rather than on its current prospects.

    Learn More

  • First Conclusion Bias

    The mind's tendency to settle on the first conclusion that comes to it, without considering alternatives. It's a major barrier to rational thinking.

    Learn More

📈 Economics Models

  • Advantage of Scale

    (Economies of Scale). The cost advantages that businesses obtain due to their scale of operation, with cost per unit of output decreasing with increasing scale.

    Learn More

  • Wealth Effect

    The tendency for people to spend more as the value of their assets (like stocks or real estate) rises, even if their income has not changed.

    Learn More

  • Opportunity Cost

    The value of the next-best alternative that was not chosen. Every decision has an opportunity cost.

    Learn More

  • Tragedy of the Commons

    A situation where individual users, acting in their own self-interest, behave contrary to the common good of all users by depleting a shared resource.

    Learn More

  • Comparative Advantage in Trade

    The ability of an economy to produce goods and services at a lower opportunity cost than that of trade partners. It explains why trade is beneficial for all parties.

    Learn More
×

📊 Markets Models

  • Technology

    Understanding how new technologies emerge, disrupt existing industries, and create new opportunities is crucial for investing and business strategy.

    Learn More

  • Competitive Destruction

    (Creative Destruction). The process of industrial mutation that incessantly revolutionizes the economic structure from within, destroying the old one, creating a new one.

    Learn More

  • Pari-Mutuel System

    A betting system where all bets of a particular type are placed together in a pool. The odds are determined by the collective wisdom (or folly) of the crowd, not a bookmaker.

    Learn More

  • Mr. Market

    An allegory created by Benjamin Graham to describe the stock market as a moody business partner who offers to buy or sell shares every day at fluctuating prices.

    Learn More

  • Cancer-Surgery Formula

    An analogy for business decisions where you must act decisively to cut out a failing part of a business (the "cancer") to save the healthy remainder, even if it's painful.

    Learn More

⚙️ Engineering Models

  • Back-up System (Redundancy)

    The inclusion of extra components which are not strictly necessary to functioning, in case of failure of other components.

    Learn More

  • Breakpoints

    A point in a system or process where it will fail or fundamentally change. Understanding where these are is key to building resilient systems.

    Learn More

  • Margin of Safety

    Building a buffer between intended performance and failure points to account for uncertainty, risk, and unforeseen events.

    Learn More

🧬 Biology Models

    The process whereby organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring. This applies to ideas, businesses, and strategies.

    Learn More

  • Feedback Loops

    A process in which the outputs of a system are circled back and used as inputs. They can be positive (amplifying) or negative (stabilizing).

    A simple but powerful tool to reduce errors and ensure consistency, especially in complex tasks. It compensates for the limits of human memory and attention.
