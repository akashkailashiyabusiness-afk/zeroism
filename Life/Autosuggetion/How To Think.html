René Descartes

I think therefore I am.

"What then am I? A thing which thinks. What is a thing which thinks? It is a thing which doubts, understands, conceives, affirms, denies, wills, and refuses, which also imagines and feels."

John Locke

Experience is the foundation of all knowledge. We are born a blank slate (tabula rasa).

"Thinking on its own is not enough. Locke believed that knowledge was founded on empirical observation and experience. There are no innate ideas that are with us when we are born."

David Hume

Causality is learned by experience, not reason.

"Hume believed causes and effects are discoverable not by reason but by experience. Even with the perspective of the past, humanity cannot dictate future events because thoughts of the past are limited compared to the possibilities for the future."

Philosophical Perspectives

Generalized views of the world that form beliefs and guide action. Click to explore the frameworks.

To Predict

Positivism: Knowledge is deductive, value-free, and relies on empirical scientific evidence.

To Understand

Interpretivism: Natural science methods cannot apply to social science; interpretations are culturally derived.

To Emancipate or Liberate

Critical Theory: Research should be used to change situations, focusing on power relations and critiquing assumptions.

To Deconstruct

Post-Modernism: Truth claims are socially constructed to serve interests; distrusts universal definitions of reality.

Foundational Concepts

Metaphysics

The study of the nature of reality.

Epistemology

The study of being and knowledge.

Ontology

What exists in the human world that we can acquire knowledge about?

Reality Paradigms

Realism: One reality exists.

Naïve Realism: Reality can be understood using appropriate methods.
Critical Realism: Reality captured by broad critical examination.

Relativism: Multiple realities exist.

Realities exist as multiple, intangible mental constructions; no reality beyond subjects.
