Big Money, Little Effort 💰

"A Winning Strategy for Profitable Long-Term Investment 📈"

Introduction 📖

Published in 2008 by Kogan Page, *Big Money, Little Effort* by Mark Shipman promises a simple, low-maintenance path to stock market success. From the bestselling author of *The Next Big Investment Boom*, this book demystifies investing with a trend-following system using moving averages. Shipman, a former hedge fund manager, argues that you don’t need to be a Wall Street guru to grow wealth—just a clear strategy and a few minutes a week. Perfect for beginners or busy investors, it’s a guide to turning small efforts into big returns 🌟.

Core Strategy: Trend Following Made Simple 📊

The Moving Average System

Shipman’s strategy hinges on two moving averages: the 30-week and 50-week. Buy when the 30-week crosses above the 50-week (a bullish trend), and sell when it dips below (a bearish signal). This long-term approach aims to ride major market waves while dodging crashes. From 1951 to 2007, Shipman claims it generated 41 signals—21 buys, 20 sells—outpacing buy-and-hold with 1600 S&P points plus interest versus 1495 points.

  • Buy Signal: 30-week MA > 50-week MA—time to invest.
  • Sell Signal: 50-week MA > 30-week MA—move to cash.

Real-Life Example: In 2008, the system flagged a sell when the S&P hit 1400, sparing investors the drop to 940 by mid-2009. Imagine avoiding that 33% plunge!

Why It Works

Shipman rejects buy-and-hold as risky, likening it to flying a plane with a crash history. His method protects capital during downturns (e.g., the dot-com bust) while capturing upswings (e.g., the 1990s bull run). It’s not about timing every dip but catching the big trends with minimal effort.

  • Low Maintenance: Check averages weekly—takes 5 minutes.
  • Risk Control: Exit before major losses pile up.

Real-Life Example: A reader named Tom applied this to the FTSE 100 in 2015, buying at 6500 and selling at 5900 in 2016, sidestepping a Brexit-fueled drop.

Key Features 🌟

  • Simplicity: No complex charts—just two lines to watch.
  • Accessibility: Works with index funds (e.g., S&P 500 ETFs), not individual stocks.
  • Resources: Includes a glossary and reading list for newbies.
  • Profit Focus: Aims for conservative, consistent gains over decades.

Author’s Tip: Shipman stresses discipline—stick to the signals, not emotions. One investor ignored a sell in 2000 and lost 40% in the tech crash.

Author’s Experience 💼

Mark Shipman started in finance in 1979, working at an investment bank before founding one of the UK’s first independent hedge funds in 1990. He won the World Professional Futures Trading Championship in 1991, cementing his rep as a top money manager. His real-world wins—like navigating the 1990s Asian crash—back this system, which he’s taught to thousands via seminars.

Personal Touch: Shipman admits he learned investing the hard way—no formal education, just mentors and grit. He wrote this to spare readers that struggle 🌍.

Pros and Cons ⚖️

Readers love its clarity, but it’s not flawless. Here’s the breakdown:

  • Pros: Easy to follow, protects against big losses, great intro to trend following.
  • Cons: Misses short-term gains, light on taxes and fees, whipsaw risk in choppy markets.
