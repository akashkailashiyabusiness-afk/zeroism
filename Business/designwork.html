Design Your Work: Praxis Volume 1 by Tiago Forte

A journey into the future of work through design and productivity.

Overview

"Design Your Work: Praxis Volume 1" by Tiago Forte chronicles his early exploration into the future of work, sparked by his experiences after returning from the Peace Corps in 2011 and joining a product development firm in San Francisco. This collection of 16 essays, written between 2014 and 2016 and originally published on the Praxis blog, blends design thinking, productivity insights, and personal reflections. Forte tackles the question, “What is the future of work?” by weaving together topics like meditation, habit formation, and digital workflows, offering a fresh perspective on modern productivity.

Key Concepts

Design Thinking in Work

Forte applies principles from design—learned in Silicon Valley—to reimagine how work can be structured, emphasizing creativity and problem-solving over rigid routines.

Habit Formation

Explores how small, intentional habits can reshape productivity, drawing from personal experiments and broader theories to create sustainable workflows.

Digital Tools as Extensions

Introduces the idea of using tools like Evernote to extend cognitive capacity, laying early groundwork for concepts later expanded in "Building a Second Brain."

Lessons

  • Adaptability: Embrace flexibility in work design to thrive amidst rapid technological and cultural shifts.
  • Mindfulness: Use practices like meditation to enhance focus and emotional resilience, as Forte learned from a Vipassana retreat.
  • Workflow Optimization: Streamline tasks with digital tools and intentional systems, such as email management techniques.
  • Curiosity: Approach productivity with an experimental mindset, testing and refining methods to suit personal needs.

Life Examples

Redesigning a Morning Routine

Apply habit formation by pairing meditation with journaling in Evernote, creating a calm, focused start to the day.

Managing a Startup Project

Use design thinking to prototype a product launch, iterating based on feedback, and organize notes digitally for quick access.

Balancing Work and Life

Implement a gratitude practice (inspired by Forte’s essays) alongside a task system to maintain clarity and motivation.
