Good Leaders Ask Great Questions Summary

John Maxwell on the Power of Inquiry

Introduction

John Maxwell argues that effective leadership hinges on the ability to ask insightful questions. Asking the right questions can unlock potential, foster collaboration, and drive positive change.

Key Concepts

The Importance of Asking Questions

Maxwell emphasizes that asking questions is not a sign of weakness but rather a demonstration of strength and wisdom. Questions help leaders to understand situations, gather information, and empower their teams.

Real-life Example: A leader facing a challenging project might ask their team, "What are the potential roadblocks we might encounter?" rather than simply dictating a solution.

Actionable Steps:

  • Practice active listening. Pay attention to what others are saying and ask follow-up questions.
  • Create a "question journal." Keep a notebook of thought-provoking questions.

Types of Questions

The book explores various types of questions, including questions for:

  • Clarification: "Can you explain that further?"
  • Connection: "How does this relate to our overall goals?"
  • Creativity: "What are some alternative approaches?"
  • Evaluation: "What are the potential risks and rewards?"
  • Action: "What are the next steps?"

Real-life Example: A leader seeking to innovate might ask, "What if we tried this completely different approach?"

Actionable steps: Prepare a list of questions for your next team meeting, ensuring that you include questions from each category.

Creating a Culture of Inquiry

Maxwell highlights the importance of fostering a culture where asking questions is encouraged and valued. This requires creating a safe space for people to express their thoughts and ideas without fear of judgment.

Real-life Example: A leader can start meetings by asking, "What questions do you have?" This creates an environment where questions are expected and welcomed.

Actionable steps: Actively solicit questions from your team. Reward those who ask thoughtful questions. Make it clear that asking questions is essential for learning and growth.
